THE PROBLEM(s)

13% OF THE WORLD'S POPULATION

do not have access to electricity. An estimated 1 billion people.

(Source: International Energy Agency)

As surprising as the statement above is, there is more...

MORE THAN 1 BILLION

people cook using polluting open fires or simple stoves

(Source: International Energy Agency)

4 MILLION PEOPLE A YEAR

die prematurely from illnesses attributable to household air pollution

(Source: United Nations Environment Programme)

DESPITE ONGOING EFFORTS, IN 20 YEARS

there could still be hundreds of millions of people without electricity

(Source: International Energy Agency)

As surprising as the above statements are, there is even more...

11% OF THE WORLD'S POPULATION

lack access to safe water. An estimated 840 million people.

(Source: World Health Organization)

200 MILLION hours a day

are spent by women and girls searching for unhealthy water

(Source: United Nations International Children's Emergency Fund)

1.8 MILLION children under the age of 5

die annually from water-borne illnesses

(Source: World Health Organization)

DESPITE ONGOING EFFORTS, WATER SCARCITY

over the next decade will be the largest global risk in terms of potential impact

(Source: World Economic Forum)

The statistics above are just a few that speak of the realities that plague daily life for those that live in areas of great need. While aid provides temporary relief for many areas, the reality is that far too many communities have not been reached.
